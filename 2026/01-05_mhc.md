# mHC: Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections

* Authors: DeepSeek
* Link: https://www.arxiv.org/abs/2512.24880
* Date: 2025-12-31

## Notes

* Hyper-Connections (HC): extends the residual connection by expanding the residual stream width and diversifying
  connectivity patterns; performance gain, but training instability, restricted scalability, memory access overhead
* Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC): project the residual connection space of HC onto a specific manifold to
  restore the identity mapping property
* Utilize the Sinkhorn-Knopp algorithm to entropically project H_res (learnable mapping that mixes features within the
  residual stream) onto the Birkhoff polytope, effectively constrains the residual connection matrices within the
  manifold that is constituted by doubly stochastic matrices
* Since the row and column sums of these matrices equal to 1, the operation H_res*x functions as a convex
  combination of the input features; the feature mean is conserved, and the signal norm is strictly regularized,
  mitigating the risk of vanishing or exploding signals
* Due to the closure of matrix multiplication for doubly stochastic matrices, the composite mapping retains this
  conservation property, maintaining the stability of identity mappings between arbitrary depths
