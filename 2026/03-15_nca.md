# Training Language Models via Neural Cellular Automata

* Authors: MIT
* Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10055
* Date: 2026-03-09

## Notes

* use neural cellular automata (NCA) to generate synthetic, non-linguistic data for pre-pre-training
* NCA data exhibits rich spatiotemporal structure and statistics resembling natural language while being controllable
  and cheap to generate at scale
* pre-pre-training on only 164M NCA tokens improves downstream language modeling by up to 6% and accelerates convergence
  by up to 1.6x
* outperforms pre-pre-training on 1.6B tokens of natural language from Common Crawl with more compute
* gains transfer to reasoning benchmarks
* attention layers are the most transferable
* optimal NCA complexity varies by domain: code benefits from simpler dynamics, math and web text favor more complex
  ones
* sample rules based on the gzip compression ratios of generated sequences; for rollouts, serialize all timesteps into a
  byte stream and compute r = compressed bytes/raw bytes ∗ 100
